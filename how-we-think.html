Philosophy

Symptoms lie.
Root causes don't.

Every problem you see is just the surface. The real problem is always deeper.

The Seven Cylinders Framework

Every organization runs on seven cylinders. When something breaks, most people fix the cylinder that's making noise. We find the cylinder that's actually broken.

  1. Strategy — Where are we actually going?
  2. Leadership — Who's driving, and are they aligned?
  3. Operations — How does work actually get done?
  4. People — Right people, right roles?
  5. Systems — What enables progress? What blocks it?
  6. Culture — What behaviors are actually rewarded?
  7. Customer — Who are we serving, and why do they choose us?

The Diagnosis-First Principle

Most consultants start with solutions. We start with diagnosis.

Why? Because solutions without diagnosis are just guesses. And guesses waste time and money.

Our methodology:

  • Listen to what's stuck (the symptom)
  • Assess all seven cylinders (the system)
  • Identify the root cause (what's actually broken)
  • Design targeted interventions (what will actually fix it)
  • Implement and measure (until it sticks)

Why This Works

Over 24 years and 50+ transformations, we've learned one thing: symptoms always lie.

Marketing problems are usually operations problems. Turnover is usually leadership problems. Poor placements are usually student readiness. Stalled revenue is usually strategy + systems.

The symptom tells you something is broken. The root cause tells you what.

The Uncomfortable Truth

Root cause diagnosis is uncomfortable. It exposes misalignment, dysfunction, and problems people have been avoiding.

That's exactly why most organizations skip it.
And exactly why nothing changes.
