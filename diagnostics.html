Common Questions

Questions before you start

How is this different from a generic AI business tool?

+
The diagnostic engine runs on a proprietary pattern database built from 25 years of real institutional case data — not publicly available training data. Generic AI tools can describe business problems. This engine diagnoses them: it walks the causal chain from your specific symptom, through observable operational patterns, to the systemic root cause. The reasoning is visible in the report. You can challenge it. That is the difference between a tool and a diagnostic.

When should I start with the free assessment vs paying for a diagnostic?

+
Start free if you want to understand whether Kernorigin's framework applies to your situation — or if you want clarity on which cylinder is your primary bottleneck before committing to anything. Move to a paid diagnostic when you need the full causal chain and a 90-day roadmap your leadership team can act on immediately, or when you are preparing for a significant strategic or investment decision. The free report will often tell you which path makes sense.

How long do the forms take to complete?

+
The free assessment takes 5–10 minutes — 10 questions about your business. The Institutional Diagnostic questionnaire takes 20–25 minutes and goes deeper across all seven cylinders. The Systems Diagnostic is 15 questions focused on process and technology — also 15–20 minutes. All forms can be paused and resumed. The report arrives by email within minutes of completion.

Who in my organisation should complete the diagnostic?

+
The founder, CEO, or Managing Director typically gets the most accurate results — they have the widest view across all seven cylinders. For the Systems Diagnostic specifically, the Head of Operations or CTO alongside the CEO produces the best output. Avoid having the diagnostic completed by a single functional head, as it creates blind spots in the cylinders outside their domain.

What if I disagree with the diagnostic findings?

+
That disagreement is often itself diagnostic. The report shows the causal reasoning behind every finding — you are not asked to accept a conclusion, you are shown the chain of evidence. Clients who initially disagree typically find, on examination, that the report has named something they knew was true but had not been willing to address. If after review you believe the report is wrong, contact us. We will schedule a call and work through the reasoning together. You own the report either way.

Is there a refund policy?

+
If you complete a paid diagnostic and believe the report does not reflect your actual business situation — not just that the findings are uncomfortable — contact us within 7 days. We will review the case and either supplement the report or discuss a refund. In five years of paid diagnostics, we have not received a refund request. We are confident enough in that record to offer it.

Can I get a diagnostic in Hindi or Kannada?

+
Yes. All three diagnostic products are available in English, Hindi, and Kannada. Select your preferred language at the start of the assessment form. The full report — including the causal chain analysis, the roadmap, and the intervention recommendations — is delivered in your chosen language.

What happens after the diagnostic?

+
The free assessment email includes a clear next step based on your findings. Paid diagnostics include a debrief call with a senior partner. After the debrief, you may be invited to the Root Cause Resolution advisory program — a 90-day structured cohort for organisations ready to implement their roadmap with expert support. Some clients proceed directly to a transformation engagement. Some take the report and implement it independently. All of these are valid outcomes. We will only recommend what your situation actually requires.
